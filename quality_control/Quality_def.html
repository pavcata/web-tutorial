Quality

Definition of quality

A high quality product has eight key aspects:

  • Reliability- how often does the product fail?
  • Durability- how long does it last?
  • Conformance -is the product made according to specification?
  • Serviceability- how easy is it to repair the product?
  • Perceived quality- what is the image of the company?
  • Features- what does the product do?
  • Performance- will the product do the intended job?
  • Aesthetics- what does the product look like?

Quality Control

Quality is inversely proportional to variability. Monitoring of the control inputs allows for process optimization. The ultimate result is a higher quality product. The feedback loop between the final product and the controllable inputs to the process ensures continuous improvement of final outputs. The quality of a product depends on product development and production engineering. During product development the design should be robust and with minimum response to noise. Hence, performance variations due to variability are reduced. At the production engineering stage, the process should produce uniform product economically. The company continues to service the product after it is handed over to the customer to maintain reputation despite any quality loss.
There are two types of quality control: on-line and off-line. On-line quality control if part of the production engineering process. It aims to keep mean values on target and maintain inherent standard deviations of the process. Off-line quality control features in product development and production engineering. It covers system design, parameter design, tolerance design, and removal of causes for defects. Some of the most common quality tools are Cause & Effect diagrams, Pareto charts, Control charts, and Check sheets.

Cause & Effect (Fishbone) Diagrams

A Cause & Effect diagram like the one on figure 2 is used to identify problems in the initial design and manufacturing process. The diagram has five branches corresponding to the five main areas that could cause a defect. Each area can be branched further to pinpoint a specific problem and understand who is responsible for it. This helps to identify the root causes of defects in the final product.

Check sheets

"The Check Sheet is a simple document that is used for collecting data in real time and at the location where the data is generated. The document is typically a blank form that is designed for the quick, easy, and efficient recording of the desired information, which can be either quantitative or qualitative. When the information is quantitative, the check sheet is sometimes called a tally sheet. The check sheet is one of the seven basic tools of quality control made popular by Dr. Kaoru Ishikawa. A defining characteristic of a check sheet is that data is recorded by making marks (“checks”) on it. A typical check sheet is divided into regions, and marks made in different regions have different significance. Data is read by observing the location and number of marks on the sheet"(2). The five types of check sheets include:

  • Classification check sheet
  • Data collection check sheet
  • Frequency check sheet
  • Measurement scale check sheet
  • Check list

Classification check sheets

The classification check sheet classifies traits such as defects into different categories. It provides a visual overview of the problem areas on different days of the week (2).

Defect location check sheet

This type of check sheet indicates the physical location of a defect on a picture. The defect location check sheet reveals an area of a product that tends to display most of the defects. Once this is known, the team can go back to the process to see what it is about the specific place of the product that is causing the defects (2).

Frequency Check Sheet

"The presence or absence of a trait or combination of traits is indicated. Also, number of occurrences of a trait on a part can be indicated"(2). If only the number of defects is tracked, then "wrong color" will not be identified as having the highest frequency of occurrence. The "wrong color" category has to be broken down to unveil that green is giving the most defects.

Measurement scale check sheet

"A measurement scale is divided into intervals and measurements are indicated by checking an appropriate interval"(2).

Check list

The items to be performed for a task are listed so that as each is accomplished it can be marked as having been completed.
